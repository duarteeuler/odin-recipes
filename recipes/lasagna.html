Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of pasta, meat or vegetables, and cheese, all baked together in a dish. It is typically made with flat, wide noodles and a variety of fillings such as ground beef, sausage, or vegetables like spinach and mushrooms. The noodles are layered with the filling and cheese, and the dish is then baked until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Lasagna is often served as a main course at meals, and can be accompanied by a variety of sides such as garlic bread, salad, or a light soup. It is a hearty, flavorful dish that is sure to satisfy any appetite.

Ingredients

  • Pasta sheets
  • Meat
  • Tomato sauce
  • Cheese
  • Vegetables
  • Spices
  • Olive oil
  • Butter
  • Milk
  • Egg

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the pasta sheets according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large pan, cook the ground meat over medium heat until it is browned. Drain any excess fat.
  4. Add the tomato sauce, vegetables, and spices to the pan with the meat and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Coat the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish with a little bit of olive oil and butter.
  6. To assemble the lasagna, spread a layer of the meat mixture on the bottom of the baking dish. Top with a layer of pasta sheets, followed by another layer of the meat mixture and a layer of shredded cheese. Repeat these layers until you have used up all of the ingredients, ending with a layer of cheese on top.
  7. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Let the lasagna rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy!
